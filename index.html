Devil's Cross

How the game is to be played

History

“Altus Disputatio” is a premier debating event conducted by The National University of Advanced Legal Studies(NUALS), Kochi. This event features the unique “Devil’s Cross” format, conceived and patented by our University. The event is a definitive feature of the Debating Circle, and has won much acclaim from participants, judges and the public alike, owing to its fast-paced rounds and unconventional method. This time, the NUALS Economics Forum, in association with the University’s Centre for Law and Economics, plans to host the sixth edition of this sui generis debate.

Overview

The debate requires sixteen teams, to be divided into four pools of four teams each- Soprano, Contralto, Baritone, and Tenor. Each team must consist of three members- an inquisitor and two speakers. Each pool will have four sessions and the team with the highest points from each pool will advance to the next round. In the penultimate round, two of these teams will be eliminated. The final round will be between the two surviving teams, one of which will be declared the winner. The competition will be judged by external judges.

Rounds Involved

Altus Disputatio consists of three rounds per game. Expository, Elocution and Entrap.

Expository requires the speakers to deliver their interpretation of the topic for a minute. The topic will be vague and the speakers may feel free to draw analogies and logical deductions from it. The inquisitor's role is to aid the judge in choosing the motion. Bonus points will be awarded if the judge goes with the suggested motion.

Elocution requires the opposition and proposition team to speak on their motion for five minutes. It is to be noted that they DO NOT GET TO PICK SIDES.

Entrap: This is where the inquisitor comes into action. There are two rounds of entrap within every debating session. The mini round and the Final round. In the mini round, the opposition speaker can ask the proposition speaker two questions to which he must answer within a minute and vice versa. In the final round, the inquisitor asks three questions to each of the teams. Points are given based on the quality of the questions. After the final entrap, the judges may ask the teams questions.

Terms and Conditions

  • Each participating team shall consist of 3 persons, no more, no less.
  • A maximum of two teams can participate from the same college. This does not include registration of a student from the college in a cross team.
  • Any change in the team composition should be brought to the knowledge of the Organising Committee on or before 7th of March 2016.
  • The teams are to register through the link provided in the Official Website.
  • Filling up the online Registration Form does not by itself entitle a team to participate in Altus Disputatio. Acceptance of registration by the Organizing Committee, payment of the Registration Fees and confirmation of receipt of the Fees before the due date are necessary for each team to be allowed to participate.
  • Registration Fees payable by each team are Rs 4,500 with accommodation and Rs 2,100 without accommodation.
  • The teams should proceed to payment only after receiving the confirmation email from the Organizing Committee of Altus Disputatio.
  • The teams are advised not to proceed to payment without receiving the confirmation email from the Organizing Committee.
  • The Account details will be provided in the confirmation mail by the Organizing Committee below the head of “Payment of Registration Fees”.
  • The payment is to be done through NEFT [National Electronic Fund Transfer], a payment option available at any Nationalized/ Scheduled Bank.
  • As a part of the process a Unique Transfer Number [UTR Number] is generated. On the transfer of the fees the UTR NUMBER must be sent as an email to the Organizing Committee.
  • Please ensure that Unique Transfer Number is correctly typed and sent as an email to altusdisputatio@nuals.ac.in . On receiving the mail, the Budget Coordinator of the Altus Disputatio Organizing Committee shall check with our Bank whether the payment has been credited.
  • If the UTR Number matches with the UTR Number supplied by the bank, the payment shall be considered successful and will be acknowledged by the Organizing Committee through mail within 24 hours of its receipt.
  • Deadline for filling up the registration form on the Official Website is 27th February 2016.
  • Registrations after the deadline shall not be accepted.
  • The registration fee once paid will NOT be refunded under any circumstances.
  • All certificates and awards will be presented to the participants at the Valedictory Ceremony. The participants are advised to finalize their travel plan accordingly.
  • All decisions made by the Organising Committee in case of any disputes, shall be final and binding on the participating teams.
