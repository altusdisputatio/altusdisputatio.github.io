The Event

What is Altus Disputatio
Conceived in 2010, Altus Disputatio is a novel debating tournament held under the aegis of the Debate Forum at the National University of Advanced Legal Studies (Nuals), Kochi. The forum has crafted a unique format titled the "Devil's Cross" which is followed in all the tournaments.Known for its unparalleled quality of debating and staggering prize money, it has gained accolades from all quarters.


Devil's Cross

How the game is to be played

Overview

The game requires sixteen teams which will be divided into four pools of four teams each- Soprano, Contralto, Baritone, Tenor. Each team must consist of three members: An inquisitor and two speakers. The pool will have four sessions and the team with the highest points will advance to the next round. In the penultimate round, two of the teams will be eliminated. The final round will be between the two surviving teams, one of which will be declared the winner. The competition will be judged by external judges.

Rounds Involved

Altus Disputatio consists of three rounds per game. Expository, Elocution and Entrap.

Expository requires the speakers to deliver their interpretation of the topic for a minute. The topic will be vague and the speakers may feel free to draw analogies and logical deductions from it. The inquisitor's role is to aid the judge in choosing the motion. Bonus points will be awarded if the judge goes with the suggested motion.

Elocution requires the opposition and proposition team to speak on their motion for three minutes. It is to be noted that they DO NOT GET TO PICK SIDES.

Entrap: This is where the inquisitor comes into action. There are two rounds of entrap within every debating session. The mini round and the Final round. In the mini round, the opposition speaker can ask the proposition speaker two questions to which he must answer within a minute and vice versa. In the final round, the inquisitor asks three questions to each of the teams. Points are given based on the quality of the questions. After the final entrap, the judges may ask the teams questions.
